TIME PERIOD
THE 50S

It was the infancy of the Rock 'n Roll era, but that doesn't mean it lacked good music. Great trees like Elvis Presley, Fats Domino, Chuck Berry or Ray Charles laid a solid foundation for the next generation with extremely rustic melodies.

THE 60S

Tie up your hair, pick up your guitar, turn the amp up to maximum as we're about to start the British Invasion. Rest assured because our forces are very large: Beatlemaniacs are a bit of an easy-to-listen Pop Rock, Floydians with the virtual Prog Rock, or the sad Stoners with the Blues. Although not contributing directly, the war in Vietnam was also the inspiration of many works of this period.

THE 70S

The boom of rock types has come. This is the first decade that is considered the Golden Era of the music industry. This includes the rapid development of recording and production technology. Turntables are produced in large quantities because of the unprecedented high demand. Everyone is "hungry" for music. Typical artists include The Carpenters, Elton John, Queen, Jackson 5,…

THE 80S

Rock is dying, Dance music is on the throne! The era of Synthesizers produced the sound that is considered characteristic of the 80s. Disco comes and goes, Funk Soul develops so that people can no longer clearly distinguish music genres from each other. And the legend of Rick Roll lived forever with the Internet from this period.

THE 90S

MTV, cassette tapes and CDs were born, making vinyl a game of traditional values. The number of vinyl records of this period is very small because of the decrease in demand, but because of that, they have a very high collectible value. Hiphop, Electronic, Punk and Alternative Rock were the popular music genres of the decade.

YEAR 20S

Music of the new generation, the new millennium.
